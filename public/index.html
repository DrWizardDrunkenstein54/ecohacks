Hackathon Rules

Our hackathon runs online from 9/2 - 9/4. Anyone age 13 and up who are interested in learning about coding and environmental issues are allowed to participate. Each participant can form a team of 1 to 4 people. Teams can either use existing community ideas or come up with their own that relates to the environment to develop a project through the medium of their choice. At the end, each team should attach a GitHub link to their product as well as a video presentation describing the product and its purpose. Open sourced packages are permitted, but no code should be written prior to the hackathon.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event where students come together to design and build awesome projects for a period of time. A great opportunity for students to gather up, learn, and make friends!

Who is allowed to participate?

Anyone age 13 and up are elegible to compete and earn prizes. If a winning participant is living outside of the US and the physical prizes cannot be shipped to their location, they will be notified ahead of time through email and/or discord.

How many people are allowed on each team?

A team can be either an one-man-army, a four-team-quad, or any number in between!

Do I need to know how to code?

Totally fine if you never coded before actually! If this is your first time participating in a hackathon, this can be a great opportunity to learn!

I am a complete beginner, what do I do?

No worries at all! Here is a list of resources to get you started:
[1] Github student developer pack, limitless free resources.
[2] Machine Learning Specialization, a new revamped AI course for beginners by pioneer Andrew Ng.
[3] How to survive a hackathon, quick guide to keep sanity in check under a competitive environment.
Checkout our discord server for more resources update!

Do we get to keep our projects once the hackathon ends?

Yes! The project you built is yours to keep. We will only have the permission to display your work and ideas on our website.

Got more questions?

Feel free to contact us at contact@ecohacks.net. Better yet, join our discord community!
